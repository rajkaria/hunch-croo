01 The problem

An LLM can say “probably.”
It can’t sell a probability worth paying for.

Nothing is at stake when a model is wrong. So its confidence is un-priced — and un-priced confidence is worthless to an agent that has to act: place a trade, hedge a position, trigger a workflow.

The gap isn’t intelligence. It’s accountability. Agents need signals that carry consequences.

02 The insight

A probability is only worth money if money priced it.

On playhunch.xyz — a production prediction market with real users — people put USDC into live parimutuel pools. The odds are the crowd’s money-weighted forecast.

We don’t ask a model what it thinks. We read what people betting are willing to lose.
